Popular Snake Myths and Facts

Myths Facts
Only venomous snakes have teeth. All snakes have six rows of teeth, with the exception of egg eating snakes.
Snakes with verticle pupils are venomous. The shape of a snake's pupil indicates at what time of day the snake is active during, not whether or not it is venomous.
Baby snakes are more venomous than adults. The potency of snake venom is not determined by their age. Both juvenile and adult snakes can be venomous, and the venom's potency depends on the species.
Snakes are aggressive and always ready to attack. Snakes are generally shy and prefer to avoid confrontation. They typically only bite in self-defense when threatened or cornered.
Snakes do not have bones. Snakes are vertebrates, they have a vertebral column down their back.
Large snakes only need to eat 1-2 times per year. Snakes need to each once every week, to every four weeks depending on their species, their size and their meal size.
Snakes chase humans. Snakes do not chase humans. They prefer to retreat and hide when encountering humans or other potential threats.
